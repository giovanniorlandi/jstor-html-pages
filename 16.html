Introduction\nIn the emergency situations, such as earthquakes, snowstorms,\nand wars, ensuring all kinds of materials to be\nefficiently dispatched is the key problem to the emergency\nlogistics. The preliminary study results show that the\noriginal version and some slightly improved versions of ACA\nare quite good in solving some simple test performances.\nWang [5] changed the dynamic vehicle routing problem into\na series of static subproblems based on time axis. Mathematic Model of Emergency Material\nDispatch in Dynamic Environment\nAs to dynamic events of DVRP, the normal treatments are\nthe event trigger mechanism and rolling horizon principle.\nWhile we adopt the event triggermechanism, the new arrived\nmaterial assignment is immediately inserted into the running\ndispatch scheme. Meanwhile we adopt the rolling horizon\nprinciple and divide the operation time into several small\nhorizons. In the intervals, the material dispatch scheme is\nadjusted. The two treatments have both advantages and disadvantages.\nThe former one may cause the frequent scheme\naltering, while the latter one may decrease the service quality.\n2.1. Strategic Analysis. Because sudden incidents in the emergency\nlogistics are various and discrete, the demand of\nmaterials is changing with the development of the situation.\nAccording to the actual material assignment operation, a\nrolling horizon multitask decision-making emergency material\ndispatch model is built based on time axis, and the\ndispatch scheme is updatedwhile distributing unfinished dispatch\nscheme and addon dispatch scheme in these intervals.\nIn the interval, the event occurrences trigger the simple\nğ¾ exchange neighborhood optimization and service denial\nstrategy, while guaranteeing that every undamaged vehicle\nfinally returns to the logistic center.
