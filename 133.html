The Pannonian-Peripannonian region extends between the rivers \nDrava, Sava and Danube, the Alpine region covers the area of the Dinaric Alps, Gorski \nKotar and Lika, whereas the Mediterranean region extends along the Adriatic coast \nand includes a multitude of islands, cliffs and ridges. The study area includes 38 localities\nbelonging to the Mediterranean, Alpine and Pannonian-Peripannonian regions of \nCroatia (Figs 1–6, Table 1).\nSpecimen sampling and identification\nThe recent sampling effort was carried out between 2004 and 2017. Most flies were \ncollected with hand nets, with additional specimens collected by Malaise traps, plastic \nbottle traps and multipurpose traps used to collect biting flies. Specimens were preserved\nin 96% ethanol or killed in ethyl acetate and pinned shortly after sampling. \nMale terminalia were extended in loco when specimens were still fresh, or male and \nfemale terminalia were dissected at a later stage following the method described by \nRichet et al. (2011). Male abdomens were removed and soaked in a 10% KOH solution\nfor 72 hours, immersed in 10% acetic acid for one minute and rinsed in water for \nanother minute before being dehydrated in beech-wood creosote for four hours; the \nphallus, pre- and postgonites, sternite 5, cerci and surstyli were separated from the rest \nof the abdomen, placed in a drop of Canada balsam on a microscope slide and covered\nwith a coverslip. Female abdomens were detached, washed in an ethanol solution \nand left to macerate in a 10% KOH solution for one hour; abdomen tips were then \nseparated by cutting the membrane between the last two tergites, and rinsed in acetic \nacid and water before being left between slide and coverslip in beech-wood creosote \nfor a period of four hours; they were then mounted in Canada balsam between two \ncoverslips. Identifications were carried out using current keys for Sarcophagidae (Pape \n1987; Povolný and Verves 1997; Richet et al. 2011) and descriptions and illustrations \nin Whitmore (2010, 2011), Whitmore et al.
